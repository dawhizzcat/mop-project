Mosquito & Organism Purification Project

Removing mosquitoes,
leaving everything else.

Mosquitoes breed on the still film of stagnant water. The MOPP system aggregates and sinks that surface algae cutting off the breeding layer while leaving the pond's biosphere intact.

01 — The issue

Surface algae is a mosquito breeding hotspot.

Standing water with a stable surface layer is a major breeding location for mosquitoes. Wherever the surface stays undisturbed and rich in algae, mosquitoes breed excessively and increase the risk of transmitted diseases like malaria and yellow fever.

Most solutions now are Larvicides, oils, and draining. These work by killing broadly or destroying the habitat, and require reapplication to stay clean. MOPP simply moves the algal layer to be inaccessible to mosquitoes, and leaves the rest of the ecosystem alone.

02 — The method

Aggregate, clear, remove.

A simple 3 step process.

  1. 01

    Aggregate

    Microbubbles from the splitting of H20 molecules in electrolysis cause algal particles and cells to aggregate into large groups.

  2. 02

    Clear

    The aggregated algal content sinks further down the water column, leaving the surface of the water clear of algae and film.

  3. 03

    Expose

    Without the biofilm to attach to nor their primary food source, mosquito larvae die off.

MOPP targets the physical breeding layer, nothing in the water is chemically killed or harmed.
