01 — The problem

A film of algae is a mosquito nursery.

Standing water with a stable surface layer is where mosquitoes lay and raise their young. Larvae hang at that film to breathe. Wherever the surface stays undisturbed and rich in algae, the breeding cycle keeps turning — and with it, the diseases mosquitoes carry.

The usual fixes are blunt. Larvicides, oils, and draining all work by killing broadly or destroying the habitat, taking the frogs, insects, and plants down with the mosquitoes. That's the trade-off MOPP was built to remove: break the breeding layer without breaking the ecosystem.

02 — The method

Aggregate, clear, expose.

A genuine three-step sequence — the surface is reorganized, not poisoned.

  1. 01

    Aggregate

    Surface algae and organic film are drawn together into concentrated clumps rather than dispersed across the whole surface.

  2. 02

    Clear

    As the film consolidates, open channels of clean water form. The continuous breeding surface larvae depend on is broken up.

  3. 03

    Expose

    Without a stable film to anchor to, larvae lose their breathing surface and the mosquito cycle collapses — while fish, plants, and other organisms carry on unharmed.

MOPP targets the physical breeding surface, not the organisms living in the water. Nothing in the pond is chemically killed.
